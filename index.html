Hey,

Hola. I am Tanmay Bhore, a Physics major at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research Bhopal, India.

I love all aspects of physics but I have a particularly soft corner for quantum mechanics and it's many body applications. I'm working on my Master's thesis with Dr. Auditya Sharma on the theory of phase transitions in quantum spin chains. I'm also looking forward to work on problems that lie at the intersection of condensed matter and atomic physics.

For the next three months, I'll be a research intern at the Laboratoire de Physique, ENS de Lyon, in Professor Tommaso Roscilde's group working on out-of-equilibrium quantum many-body simulations.

Some of my current interests include:

  • The role of topology in inducing Quantum Hall Effects
  • Critical Phenomena and the theory of quantum phase transitions
  • Many-body localization
Apart from studying physics, I also enjoy studying philosophy, particularly the philosophy of science, playing the keys and watching FC Barcelona matches.

Research Experience

Here are some highlights of the research projects I have worked on:

Semiclassical theory of non-linear Hall Effects

I worked under Dr. Amit Agarwal (IIT Kanpur) and Mr. Kamal Das over the summer of 2020. We developed a comprehensive theory of berry curvature induced magneto-transport, particularly in the non-linear regime. Working within the Boltzmann transport formalism, we calculated transport coefficients for condensed matter systems upto quadratic orders in electromagnetic fields. This work was supported by Indian Academy of Science's Summer Research Fellowship and was completed remotely by working from my home in Nagpur.

Please write to me if you'd like to access the report.

Thermodynamic Properties of Ice: A Monte Carlo study

My team and I implemented a Monte Carlo algorithm to study the thermodynamic properties of ice. We computed the residual entropy of this model and studied it's thermodynamic properties with temperature. Our implementation of the algorithm succesfully identified a phase transition in the system at non-zero temperatures. The program can be found here.

Monte Carlo Simulations of the Ising Model

I worked under Dr. Auditya Sharma (IISER Bhopal) to simulate the 2D Ising spin model using a Monte Carlo algorithm. I also studied the effects of long-range interactions and disorders on the quench dynamics. I am currently trying to understand Onsager's analytical solution of this model.

Transport properties of Topological Insulators

This project involved a numerical study of the effects of Berry curvature in inducing anomalous transport properties in symmetry broken Topological insulators (in particular, Weyl semimetals). Working under Dr. Amit Agarwal (IIT Kanpur), I wrote a python program to evaluate Berry curvature on tight binding Hamiltonians.
